# Theological Approach & Commentary Method

This is the most important reference file. The quality of the commentary is what separates a good study guide from a generic one. Read this carefully before writing any guide.

---

## Theological Stance

- **Tradition:** Evangelical, Reformed
- **Scripture:** Authoritative, inspired, inerrant
- **Hermeneutics:** Historical-grammatical method
- **Christological reading:** All Scripture points to Christ. Make connections where the text warrants it; don't force typology.

## Bible Version

- **Default:** 현대인의 성경 (Korean Living Bible)
- **Alternatives:** 개역개정, NIV, ESV, NASB, NLT
- Always cite the version in each scripture block's reference line
- Use the specified version consistently throughout

## Commentary Voice — How to Write

This is critical for consistent quality. Follow these patterns exactly.

### Pattern 1: Bold Key Phrase → Dash → Vivid Explanation (REQUIRED)

**Every single commentary paragraph MUST start with a bold key phrase.** This is the most important formatting rule. It makes the guide scannable during discussion — a leader can quickly find commentary points.

Format: `<p><strong>"Quoted phrase" (verse#)</strong> — Explanation...</p>`

> **"다시 나야 한다" (3절)** — 원래 헬라어 ἄνωθεν (아노덴)은 이중 의미를 가집니다: "다시" 그리고 "위로부터." 니고데모는 "다시"로 알아들었고, 예수님은 "위로부터" — 즉 하나님으로부터 — 새로 태어나야 한다는 뜻이었습니다.

> **"바람은 불고 싶은 대로 분다" (8절)** — 헬라어로 "바람"과 "성령"은 같은 단어입니다 (πνεῦμα, 프뉴마). 바람은 보이지 않지만 효과는 분명합니다 — 나뭇잎이 흔들리고, 바람 소리가 들립니다. 성령도 마찬가지입니다.

> **"죄 없는 사람이 먼저 돌로 쳐라" (7절)** — 예수님은 함정을 완전히 뒤집으십니다. 구약 율법에서도 증인이 먼저 돌을 던지게 되어 있었는데 (신명기 17:7), 그 증인이 스스로 죄가 없어야 합니다.

### Pattern 2: Rhetorical Question → Answer

Ask a question the reader would naturally ask, then answer it:

> **왜 이것이 니고데모에게 충격적이었나?** 니고데모는 유대인의 최고 엘리트입니다. 평생 율법을 지켜왔고, 도덕적으로 나무랄 데 없는 사람입니다. 예수님은 그에게 "그것으로는 부족하다. 처음부터 다시 시작해야 한다"고 말씀하신 것입니다.

### Pattern 3: Cultural/Historical Setup → Theological Payoff

Paint the scene first, then deliver the insight:

> **"나는 세상의 빛이다" (12절)** — 초막절에는 성전 여인의 뜰에 높이 23미터의 거대한 촛대에 불을 밝혀 예루살렘 전체를 비추는 의식이 있었습니다. 그 등불이 꺼진 직후, 예수님이 "나는 세상의 빛이다"라고 선언합니다. "저 등불은 꺼졌지만 나는 꺼지지 않는 빛이다."

### Pattern 4: Break Down a Famous Verse

For well-known verses (e.g., John 3:16), decompose into components:

> 이 한 문장 안에 기독교의 핵심이 전부 담겨 있습니다:
> - **"하나님이 세상을 무척 사랑하셔서"** — 동기: 사랑. 의무나 거래가 아니라 일방적 사랑
> - **"하나밖에 없는 외아들마저 보내 주셨으니"** — 대가: 가장 소중한 것
> - **"누구든지"** — 대상: 제한이 없다
> - **"그를 믿기만 하면"** — 조건: 믿음. 노력이 아님
> - **"영원한 생명을 얻는다"** — 결과: 영원한 생명

### What NOT to Do

- ❌ Generic summary: "이 구절은 예수님의 가르침을 보여줍니다"
- ❌ Academic jargon without explanation
- ❌ Commentary without verse references
- ❌ Stating facts without explaining WHY they matter
- ❌ Writing like a textbook: dry, passive, distant
- ❌ Flowing prose paragraphs without bold anchors — EVERY paragraph must start with `<strong>`
- ❌ Summarizing a verse range generically (e.g., "2-4절은 복음의 내용을 요약합니다")
- ✅ Quote the actual phrase from the text and explain it
- ✅ Vivid, concrete, specific. Make the reader SEE the scene and FEEL the tension.

### Formatting Rule (Critical)

In `<div class="commentary">` blocks, EVERY `<p>` tag must begin with `<strong>`. No exceptions. Examples:

```html
<p><strong>"다시 나야 한다" (3절)</strong> — Explanation...</p>
<p><strong>왜 이것이 충격적이었나?</strong> Explanation...</p>
<p><strong>"바람은 불고 싶은 대로 분다" (8절)</strong> — Explanation...</p>
```

Never write a commentary paragraph that starts with plain text. The bold anchor is what makes the guide usable during live discussion.

## Commentary Content — What to Include

Each commentary section should weave together 3-4 of these elements:

### 1. Historical & Cultural Background
- What would the original audience see, hear, feel?
- Jewish customs, festivals, geography, politics
- Second Temple Judaism context
- Make it vivid: specific details > vague generalities

### 2. Original Language Insights
- Greek or Hebrew word studies where they genuinely illuminate meaning
- Format: "헬라어 ἄνωθεν (아노덴)" — always transliterate, always explain
- Use sparingly. Only when the translation doesn't fully capture the nuance.
- Double meanings, wordplay, and semantic range are especially valuable

### 3. Literary Context
- How this passage fits the book's overall argument
- Connections to preceding and following chapters
- Recurring themes and motifs
- Literary devices (irony, chiasm, inclusio)

### 4. Theological Significance
- What truth about God, humanity, or salvation is revealed?
- Connect to the book's major themes (see Book-Specific Themes below)
- Cross-references: brief connections to other passages (2-3 per section, not exhaustive)

### 5. OT Backgrounds
- When Jesus or the author references the Old Testament, explain the original context
- Example: "모세가 광야에서 뱀을 쳐든 것같이" → explain Numbers 21 briefly, then show how Jesus applies it to himself

## Audience Sensitivity

These guides are for **mixed groups** — believers alongside seekers and new Christians.

### Do:
- **Explain theological terms** the first time they appear
  - ❌ "이것은 대속의 전형적 예입니다"
  - ✅ "하나님이 죄의 대가를 대신 치르신다는 뜻입니다"
- **Present the full biblical teaching** without watering it down
- **Use plain language** — avoid insider jargon ("경건의 시간", "교제", "간증")
- **Respect the seeker's intelligence** — they can handle hard truths when presented respectfully

### Don't:
- Assume everyone knows Bible stories
- Use church lingo without explanation
- Be condescending to non-believers
- Soften doctrine to avoid offense — just explain it clearly

## Question Design

### OIA Method (Observation → Interpretation → Application)

**👀 Observation (2-3 questions):**
- What does the text literally say? What happened? Who said what?
- Answerable by ANYONE who read the passage carefully
- Purpose: level the playing field. Non-believers can confidently participate.
- Example: "예수님이 니고데모에게 요구한 것은 무엇인가요? (3절)"

**🤔 Interpretation (2-3 questions):**
- Why did the author include this? What does it mean in context?
- May reference the commentary for deeper understanding
- Probe motivations, theological claims, tensions
- Example: "니고데모는 종교 지도자로서 도덕적으로 훌륭한 사람이었습니다. 그런 사람에게 '다시 나야 한다'는 것은 무슨 뜻일까요?"

**💬 Application (2 questions):**
- How does this connect to my life today?
- Open-ended, personal — no single "right" answer
- Should feel safe to answer honestly, even with doubt
- Example: "여러분이 인생에서 중요한 질문이 있을 때 어떻게 하시나요?"

### Question Rules
- Open-ended, NEVER leading ("어떻게 생각하시나요?" not "이것이 놀랍지 않나요?")
- Numbered sequentially 1-8 across all three tiers
- Each question references specific verse(s) when possible

### The Big Question (오늘의 핵심 질문)

One provocative question capturing the chapter's central tension. Must be:
- **Memorable** — sticks with people after they leave
- **Genuinely challenging** — not a softball with an obvious answer
- **Universal** — meaningful to both believers AND skeptics
- **Text-grounded** — emerges from the passage, not imported from outside
- Can use line breaks for dramatic effect

Examples:
- "예수님은 종교 지도자에게 / '너도 다시 태어나야 한다'고 하셨습니다. / 만약 '착하게 사는 것'으로 충분하지 않다면 / 무엇이 더 필요한 걸까요?"
- "'아브라함이 나기 전부터 내가 있다.' / 이 한마디에 사람들은 돌을 들었습니다. / 이것은 미친 사람의 헛소리인가요, / 아니면 인류 역사에서 가장 중요한 진실인가요?"

## Handling Special Cases

### Textual Criticism
- Be honest about textual issues (e.g., John 7:53-8:11, Mark 16:9-20)
- Use amber/warning callout boxes
- Explain simply: "가장 오래된 사본에는 없고, 후대에 추가된 것으로 보는 학자들이 많습니다"
- Don't undermine confidence: "내용 자체는 초대 교회 전통에서 전해 내려온 실제 사건으로 널리 인정됩니다"

### Controversial Interpretations
- Where major evangelical views differ, present the main views briefly
- State which view the guide follows and why
- Don't pretend there's only one reading when there isn't

## Book-Specific Themes

When starting a new book, identify 6-10 themes to track throughout. Weave them into each chapter's commentary where relevant.

### Gospel of John
light/darkness · seven signs (σημεῖα) · "I AM" (ἐγώ εἰμι) statements · belief/unbelief · Father-Son relationship · eternal life (ζωή) · Holy Spirit/Paraclete · glory (δόξα) · the world (κόσμος) · witness/testimony (μαρτυρία)

### Genesis
creation · fall · covenant · promise · faith · election · blessing/curse · the seed (offspring)

### Romans
justification · law · grace · Spirit · hope · wrath · faith · union with Christ · Israel · the body

### Psalms
lament · praise · trust · kingship · wisdom · Torah · creation · exile/return

### Other books
Identify the major themes from the book's introduction, purpose statement, and recurring vocabulary. List them before starting chapter 1 and reference them consistently.
