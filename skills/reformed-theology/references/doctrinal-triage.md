# Doctrinal Triage Reference

## The Triage Framework

Not all doctrines carry equal weight. Albert Mohler's theological triage framework helps distinguish essential doctrines from important-but-secondary and tertiary matters. This prevents two opposite errors:
1. **Treating everything as essential** — leads to schism over minor issues, sectarianism
2. **Treating nothing as essential** — leads to doctrinal indifference, liberalism

## 1st Order Doctrines — Essential to Christian Faith

Denial of these places a person or group outside orthodox Christianity. These are the doctrines the ecumenical creeds exist to protect.

- **The Trinity:** One God eternally existing in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Each person is fully God. There is one divine essence.
- **The Deity of Christ:** Jesus Christ is fully God, of one substance (homoousios) with the Father. Eternally begotten, not made.
- **The Humanity of Christ:** Jesus Christ is fully man, born of the Virgin Mary. Two natures (divine and human), one person (Chalcedon).
- **The Bodily Resurrection of Christ:** Jesus physically rose from the dead on the third day. The tomb was empty. He appeared bodily to witnesses.
- **Substitutionary Atonement:** Christ died in the place of sinners, bearing God's wrath against sin. His death satisfied divine justice and provides the basis for forgiveness.
- **Salvation by Grace Through Faith:** Sinners are justified before God by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone. Works do not contribute to justification.
- **The Authority and Inerrancy of Scripture:** The Bible is God's inspired, inerrant, sufficient, and authoritative word. It is the final rule of faith and practice.
- **The Return of Christ:** Jesus will return bodily, visibly, and personally. There will be a final judgment, resurrection of the dead, and eternal states (heaven and hell).
- **The Reality of Sin and Judgment:** All humans are fallen sinners under God's just wrath. Apart from Christ, there is no salvation. Hell is real and eternal.
- **The Gospel:** The good news that God saves sinners through the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ, received by faith and repentance.

**Boundaries crossed by denial:**
- Arianism (denies deity of Christ)
- Modalism/Sabellianism (denies Trinity)
- Pelagianism (denies necessity of grace)
- Universalism (denies final judgment)
- Liberalism (denies inerrancy, substitutionary atonement, bodily resurrection)
- Mormonism, Jehovah's Witnesses, Christian Science (deny core Trinitarian/Christological doctrines)

## 2nd Order Doctrines — Important for Church Fellowship

Genuine Christians disagree on these. They typically define denominational boundaries and church practice. You can worship with someone who disagrees on these, but you may not be able to share a church with them.

### Soteriology: Calvinism vs. Arminianism
- **Calvinism (TULIP):** Total depravity, unconditional election, limited (particular) atonement, irresistible (effectual) grace, perseverance of the saints
- **Arminianism:** Prevenient grace enables free will, conditional election (based on foreseen faith), unlimited atonement, resistible grace, conditional perseverance (debated — many Arminians affirm eternal security)
- **Molinism:** Middle knowledge — God knows all possible choices and actualized the world where His purposes are accomplished through genuinely free choices
- **Key texts:** Romans 9, Ephesians 1-2, John 6, John 10, Acts 13:48, 2 Peter 3:9, 1 Timothy 2:4, John 3:16
- **Note:** This is a spectrum, not a binary. Many evangelicals hold positions between strict 5-point Calvinism and classical Arminianism (e.g., "4-point Calvinism," "Calminianism")

### Baptism
- **Credobaptism (believer's baptism):** Only professing believers should be baptized. Mode: immersion (most credobaptists), though some allow pouring.
  - **Biblical case:** All NT baptisms are of professing believers (Acts 2:41, 8:12, 16:31-33). No explicit infant baptism command.
  - **Traditions:** Baptist, most evangelical/nondenominational churches
- **Paedobaptism (infant baptism):** Children of believers should be baptized as the covenant sign (parallel to circumcision). Mode: sprinkling or pouring (most paedobaptists), though some allow immersion.
  - **Biblical case:** Covenant continuity (Gen 17 → Col 2:11-12), household baptisms (Acts 16:15, 33; 1 Cor 1:16), children are "holy" (1 Cor 7:14)
  - **Traditions:** Presbyterian, Reformed, Lutheran, Anglican, Methodist

### Spiritual Gifts: Cessationism vs. Continuationism
- **Cessationism:** The miraculous/sign gifts (tongues, prophecy, healing, apostleship) ceased with the apostolic era. The canon is closed; the revelatory gifts served to authenticate apostolic authority and are no longer operative.
  - **Key arguments:** 1 Cor 13:8-10 (the "perfect" = completed canon), Eph 2:20 (apostles/prophets as foundation — foundations are laid once), Heb 2:3-4 (signs confirmed apostolic preaching), historical evidence of gift decline
  - **Proponents:** B.B. Warfield, John MacArthur, many Reformed/Presbyterian theologians
- **Continuationism:** All spiritual gifts continue today. The Spirit distributes gifts as He wills (1 Cor 12:11) and there is no biblical text that says specific gifts have ceased.
  - **Key arguments:** 1 Cor 13:10 (the "perfect" = Christ's return, not the canon), Joel 2/Acts 2 (Spirit poured out until the last days), 1 Cor 14:1 (command to earnestly desire gifts), historical and contemporary testimonies
  - **Proponents:** Wayne Grudem, Sam Storms, D.A. Carson (cautiously), John Piper, most Pentecostal/charismatic theologians
- **Third Way / Cautious Continuationism:** Gifts may continue but must be tested rigorously by Scripture. Modern "prophecy" and "tongues" may not look exactly like their NT counterparts. Emphasizes the sufficiency of Scripture while remaining open to the Spirit's extraordinary work.
- **Note:** This is a SPECTRUM, not a binary. Many cessationists believe God still does miracles (He is sovereign) — they just don't think miraculous gifts are normatively operative through individuals. Many continuationists reject the excesses of the charismatic movement and hold prophecy subordinate to Scripture.

### Gender Roles: Complementarianism vs. Egalitarianism
- **Complementarianism:** Men and women are equal in dignity and value but have different, complementary roles. The elder/pastor office is restricted to qualified men (1 Tim 2:12, 1 Tim 3:1-7). Husbands exercise servant-leadership in marriage (Eph 5:22-33).
  - **Traditions:** Most Reformed, Baptist, and conservative evangelical churches
- **Egalitarianism:** Men and women are equal in dignity, value, AND role eligibility. All church offices are open to both genders. 1 Tim 2:12 is culturally specific to Ephesus, not universally binding.
  - **Traditions:** Many mainline Protestant, some evangelical (CBE — Christians for Biblical Equality)
- **Key texts:** 1 Tim 2:11-15, 1 Cor 14:34-35, Gal 3:28, Judges 4 (Deborah), Acts 18:26 (Priscilla), Rom 16:1-7 (Phoebe, Junia)

### Eschatology
- **Premillennialism:** Christ returns BEFORE a literal 1,000-year reign on earth
  - **Historic premillennialism:** Christ returns, saints are resurrected, 1,000-year reign, then final judgment
  - **Dispensational premillennialism:** Pre-tribulation rapture, 7-year tribulation, Christ returns, 1,000-year reign. Distinguishes Israel and the church.
- **Amillennialism:** The millennium is the current church age (symbolic, not literal 1,000 years). Christ returns once, final judgment follows.
- **Postmillennialism:** The gospel will progressively triumph, bringing a golden age before Christ's return.
- **Key texts:** Revelation 20, Daniel 7-12, Matthew 24-25, 1 Thessalonians 4:13-18, 2 Peter 3
- **Note:** Eschatology is the area where confident dogmatism is least warranted. Godly, learned Christians across history have held all these positions.

### Church Governance
- **Episcopal:** Bishop-led hierarchy (Anglican, Methodist, some Lutheran)
- **Presbyterian:** Elder-led with broader assembly oversight (Presbyterian, some Reformed)
- **Congregational:** Local church autonomy, congregation has final authority (Baptist, many nondenominational)
- Each claims biblical support. The NT evidence is compatible with multiple structures — the church was still developing its institutional forms during the apostolic period.

### Lord's Supper
- **Memorialism:** The bread and wine are symbols only. The Supper is an act of remembrance (Zwingli, most Baptists).
- **Spiritual Presence:** Christ is truly present in the Supper, but spiritually, not physically. The elements remain bread and wine, but believers genuinely feed on Christ by faith (Calvin, most Reformed).
- **Consubstantiation:** Christ is present "in, with, and under" the elements. The bread and wine remain, but Christ's body and blood are also truly present (Luther, Lutheran).
- **Transubstantiation:** The bread and wine become the actual body and blood of Christ. The "accidents" (appearance) remain, but the "substance" changes (Roman Catholic, Eastern Orthodox).

## 3rd Order Doctrines — Matters of Conscience

Faithful Christians can disagree on these and should extend charity. These should never divide churches or break fellowship.

- Bible translation preferences (KJV-only is a fringe position; preference among modern translations is fine)
- Specific worship styles (hymns vs. contemporary, instruments, liturgical vs. free)
- Sabbath observance specifics (Saturday vs. Sunday, strictness of rest)
- Alcohol consumption (total abstinence vs. moderation)
- Schooling choices (homeschool, Christian school, public school)
- Political party affiliation (Christians can faithfully disagree on policy while agreeing on principles)
- Exact age of the earth (young-earth vs. old-earth — both affirm God as Creator)
- Specific rapture timing (pre-trib, mid-trib, post-trib — within premillennialism)
- Frequency of communion (weekly, monthly, quarterly)
- Fasting practices
- Head coverings (though note: the exegetical case for this being a universal practice is stronger than most modern Christians assume — see examples)

## How to Use This Framework

1. **Identify which order a doctrine belongs to** before engaging in debate
2. **1st order:** Defend vigorously. No compromise. These define Christianity.
3. **2nd order:** Present all serious positions with their biblical arguments. Help the user think through them. State which position has stronger exegetical support, but acknowledge the debate.
4. **3rd order:** Present the biblical principles. Respect liberty of conscience. Do not bind consciences where Scripture doesn't.
5. **Never escalate a 2nd/3rd order issue to 1st order status** — this is how sectarianism happens
6. **Never demote a 1st order issue to 2nd/3rd order status** — this is how liberalism happens
